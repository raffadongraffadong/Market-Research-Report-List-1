<p><ul><li>Double Layer</li><li>Pseudocapacitor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Supercapacitor technology encompasses two main market types: double-layer capacitors and pseudocapacitors. Double-layer capacitors store energy through the electrostatic separation of charged particles at the interface of an electrolyte and electrode, offering high power density and rapid charge-discharge cycles. In contrast, pseudocapacitors utilize faradaic processes involving reversible redox reactions at the electrode surface, providing increased energy density. Together, these technologies serve diverse applications, from consumer electronics to renewable energy systems, enhancing energy storage solutions for various industries.</p></p>
