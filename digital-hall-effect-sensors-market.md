<p><p>Digital Hall Effect sensors are categorized into two main types: bipolar and unipolar. Bipolar sensors detect magnetic fields of both polarities, allowing them to switch states when exposed to either north or south magnetic fields. In contrast, unipolar sensors respond only to one polarity, typically activating in the presence of a north magnetic field and remaining off with a south field. These distinctions influence their applications in various industries, including automotive, robotics, and consumer electronics, where precision and reliability are crucial.</p></p>
