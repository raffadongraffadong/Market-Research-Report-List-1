<p><ul><li>Nanofibrillated Cellulose</li><li>Nanocrystalline Cellulose</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The bacterial nanocellulose market is primarily divided into two types: nanofibrillated cellulose (NFC) and nanocrystalline cellulose (NCC). NFC is characterized by its network of fine fibers that offer enhanced mechanical properties and high surface area, making it suitable for applications in food, cosmetics, and biomedical fields. NCC, on the other hand, consists of highly crystalline nanoparticles that exhibit excellent strength and thermal stability, making it ideal for use in composites, drug delivery systems, and packaging materials.</p></p>
