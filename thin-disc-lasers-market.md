<p><p>Thin-disc lasers are categorized into three market types based on their power output: low power, mid power, and high power. Low-power thin-disc lasers typically operate below 10 watts, suitable for applications like laser engraving and small-scale material processing. Mid-power lasers, ranging from 10 to 100 watts, are used in industrial settings for cutting and marking. High-power thin-disc lasers exceed 100 watts and are employed in demanding applications such as metal welding, advanced manufacturing, and research settings, enabling precision and efficiency.</p></p>
