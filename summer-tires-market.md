<p><p>The summer tires market includes two main types: inner tube and tubeless tires. Inner tube tires feature a separate rubber tube inside the tire to hold air, offering simplicity and cost-effectiveness but requiring more maintenance. Tubeless tires, on the other hand, rely on an airtight seal between the tire and rim, providing better puncture resistance, improved fuel efficiency, and enhanced safety. Tubeless tires dominate the market due to their advanced performance and convenience, while inner tube tires remain relevant in specific applications.</p></p>
