<p><p>Bioelectronics and biosensors encompass various technologies used to detect biological changes and analyze biochemical interactions. Electrochemical biosensors utilize electrochemical methods to quantify analytes, making them popular for glucose monitoring. Thermal biosensors measure heat changes during biochemical reactions, often used in diagnostics. Piezoelectric biosensors detect mass changes based on frequency shifts, suitable for highly sensitive applications. Optical biosensors utilize light properties to analyze samples, allowing for real-time and label-free detection, widely used in environmental monitoring and clinical diagnostics.</p></p>
