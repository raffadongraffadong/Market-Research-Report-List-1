<p><ul><li>CIP Method</li><li>Vibration Molding Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Isostatically pressed graphite is produced through different techniques, primarily including the Cold Isostatic Pressing (CIP) method and vibration molding method. The CIP method involves enclosing graphite powder in a flexible mold and subjecting it to uniform high pressure, resulting in high-density products suitable for diverse applications. In contrast, vibration molding uses mechanical vibrations to compact and form the graphite powder, offering a cost-effective solution for larger production runs. Both methods serve various industries including aerospace, automotive, and electronics, highlighting their unique benefits.</p></p>
