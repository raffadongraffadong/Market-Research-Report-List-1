<p><p>Hexanedioic acid, also known as adipic acid, is primarily produced through three market types: Cyclohexane Oxidation, Cyclohexene Oxidation, and Phenol Hydrogenation. In Cyclohexane Oxidation, cyclohexane is oxidized to form adipic acid, a widely used method due to its cost-effectiveness. Cyclohexene Oxidation involves oxidizing cyclohexene, offering higher yields and fewer byproducts. Phenol Hydrogenation converts phenol into cyclohexanol, which is then oxidized to adipic acid, providing a cleaner process with reduced environmental impact. Each method caters to specific industrial needs, balancing cost, efficiency, and sustainability.</p></p>
