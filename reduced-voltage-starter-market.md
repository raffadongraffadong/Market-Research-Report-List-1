<p><p>A Reduced Voltage Starter (RVS) is an electrical device designed to start AC induction motors by reducing the voltage applied to the motor during startup. This technique minimizes inrush current, reduces mechanical stress on the motor and connected equipment, and prevents voltage dips in the supply network. Common types of RVS include autotransformer starters, star-delta starters, and soft starters, each serving specific applications and providing efficient control of motor operations.</p><p>The Reduced Voltage Starter Market is expected to experience significant growth, driven by increasing demand for energy efficiency and advancements in industrial automation. Industries such as manufacturing, oil and gas, and mining are increasingly adopting RVS technologies to enhance operational efficiency and reduce power consumption. <p><p>The reduced voltage starter market is divided into two main types: single-phase and three-phase reduced voltage starters. Single-phase reduced voltage starters are designed for small motors typically found in domestic or light industrial applications, easing startup by reducing voltage and preventing electrical surges. In contrast, three-phase reduced voltage starters are used for larger industrial motors, allowing for smoother acceleration and reduced mechanical stress. Both types enhance energy efficiency and prolong equipment lifespan by mitigating initial electrical loads during startup.</p></p>
