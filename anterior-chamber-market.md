<p><p>The anterior chamber market for intraocular lenses includes three main types: PMMA (Polymethyl Methacrylate), Silicone, and Hydrophobic Acrylate. PMMA lenses are known for their durability and stability but are less flexible. Silicone lenses offer increased flexibility and ease of implantation, making them suitable for various surgical techniques. Hydrophobic Acrylate lenses provide high optical quality and are resistant to protein deposits, making them popular for their long-term clarity and minimal complications in cataract surgery.</p></p>
