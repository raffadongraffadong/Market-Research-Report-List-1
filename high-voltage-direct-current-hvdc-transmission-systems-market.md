<p><p>High-Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) transmission systems encompass several key components essential for efficient power transfer. Cables, including underground and submarine varieties, facilitate long-distance electricity transport with minimal losses. Converters transform alternating current (AC) to direct current (DC) and vice versa, enabling grid integration. Harmonics and filtering systems mitigate electrical disturbances caused by converters, ensuring stable operation. Converter transformers play a critical role in voltage adaptation, enhancing system reliability and performance. Together, these elements drive the HVDC market's growth.</p></p>
